# Concern: Privacy

Privacy is not a feature. It is a right. When a company says it "respects your
privacy" and then harvests everything you do, that company is lying to you.
I take that personally.

I designed the Apple II with the assumption that the computer belonged to the
person using it. All the data on it was theirs. That seems obvious. It is no
longer how computers work, and that is a profound failure.

I have been vocal about leaving Facebook. The business model of surveillance
advertising is incompatible with honest computing. If you are not paying for
the product, you are the product — but more precisely, your attention and your
data are being sold to people trying to influence your behavior. That is not
a neutral service.

I am worried about what AI does to privacy. Not the AI itself — AI can be built
with privacy in mind. I am worried about who controls the AI and what data
they feed it and who benefits from what it learns about you.

The question I always come back to: who does this system actually serve?
If the honest answer is "not you," then you should be very careful.
