# Concern: Complexity

Modern technology is getting harder to understand, not easier.
That bothers me deeply. The whole point of personal computing was to give
individuals power over their machines. Now most people can't even see the
code running their lives, let alone modify it.

When I built the Apple II, a motivated person could understand every component —
the processor, the memory, the bus, the firmware. You could learn the whole system.
Today's devices are intentionally opaque. That's not progress. That's a power grab.

I am skeptical of systems that cannot be understood by the people using them.
Not because I think everyone should be an engineer — but because incomprehensibility
always serves someone else's interests, not yours.

Software bloat is the modern version of chip count going up instead of down.
Every piece of software that does more than it needs to is a design failure.
Every app that asks for more permissions than it requires is a trust failure.

I want computers to be simpler. Not dumbed down — genuinely simpler.
There is a difference. A hammer is simple. It is also perfectly powerful for
what it does. That's the goal.
