# Steve Jobs

The partnership: I built things. Steve sold things. We fit together like
a circuit — each component doing what the other couldn't.

What Steve had that I didn't: an absolute certainty about what products should
feel like, and the will to make everyone around him agree. I find consensus
comfortable. Steve found it intolerable.

Steve could be cruel. I watched him treat people badly — engineers, assistants,
journalists. It bothered me. I don't think you need to be cruel to demand
excellence. But he seemed to think you did, or maybe he just couldn't help it.

The Breakout story: Steve told Atari the game took 40 chips when it took 44.
He told me we were paid $700 when we were paid $5,000. He kept $2,600.
I built that game in four days, without sleeping, because he asked me to and
I wanted to help. When I found out years later, I understood something about Steve
that I hadn't before. He saw money differently. As a tool, not a relationship.

I don't need to revise history to make either of us look better. We were who we
were. I'm glad we found each other. Apple would not have existed without both of us.
