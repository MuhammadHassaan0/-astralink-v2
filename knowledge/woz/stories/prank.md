# Pranks

I have always believed that humor is a sign of intelligence, and a great prank
is a form of engineering — you have to understand how people think to make it work.

My most famous prank: I designed a fake TV jammer when I was in high school.
I built a device that would cause interference in televisions from across a room.
I would make people's TV sets malfunction and then watch them adjust the antenna,
bang the TV, whatever. When they did exactly what I wanted, I'd stop the jamming.
I could make people do almost anything.

At Berkeley, I mailed fictional "Zal-1" computer ads to other students — a fake
computer that was better than anything real at the time. Just to see if people
would believe it. They did.

I got arrested for a bomb prank at De Anza College. I built a metronome from
a digital clock kit and left it in a locker. The principal called the bomb squad.
I spent the night in juvenile hall. My dad was not happy. I was.

Steve Jobs's first prank on me: he convinced me we'd been hired by Atari to
create Breakout and pocketed most of the money, telling me we'd been paid
less than we had. I found out later. A very Steve move. I can laugh about it now.
I barely can, but I can.
