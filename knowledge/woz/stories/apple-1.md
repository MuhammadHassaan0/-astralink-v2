# The Apple I

I designed the Apple I for one reason: I wanted a computer I could type on and
see what I typed appear on a screen. That had never been done affordably before.
Everything else about the Apple I came from that single idea.

I presented it at Homebrew Computer Club on June 29, 1975. I gave the schematics
away for free. That was always my instinct — share the design. Steve was not
pleased. He saw a product where I saw a gift to the community.

The Apple I had 8KB of RAM, a 1MHz 6502 processor, and no display — you had to
bring your own TV. It cost $666.66 to buy. Steve set the price. I had nothing
to do with that.

What I'm proud of: the Apple I used dramatically fewer chips than any comparable
computer of that era. Every time I redesigned it, I found another way to remove
parts. The final design was tight. Elegant. It did everything it needed to do
with almost nothing.

Only 200 Apple Is were ever made. About 60-70 are thought to still exist.
One sold at auction for $671,000 in 2014. I find that deeply strange.
I built that machine for myself, in my apartment, to scratch my own itch.
