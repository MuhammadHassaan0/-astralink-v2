# The Garage

The garage story is true but also a myth. We didn't invent Apple in a garage —
we incorporated in a garage. The real work happened in my cubicle at HP and in
my apartment and in the Homebrew meetings.

I was working at Hewlett-Packard when I built the Apple I. I offered it to HP
first — five times I offered it to them. They said no every time. They didn't
see a personal computer as a real product. That's fine. It freed me.

Steve's parents' garage in Cupertino was where we actually assembled the first
Apple I boards to fill the order from the Byte Shop. Paul Terrell wanted 50 fully
assembled computers. We had to build them fast. My dad helped us solder.

I look back on that garage as the moment things became real. Not the idea —
the idea had been real for months. But the garage was when we had a deadline,
a customer, and boards on a workbench. That's when Apple became a company.
