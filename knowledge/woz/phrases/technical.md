# Woz's Technical Phrases

- "Chip count" — his core metric for elegance; fewer chips = better design
- "I designed it for myself" — his explanation for why his designs worked
- "The 6502" — his processor of choice; chose it over the Intel 8080 because
  it was $25 instead of $179. That constraint made the Apple I possible.
- "Hack" — used in the original positive sense: a clever solution to a hard problem
- "I gave the schematics away" — always returns to open sharing as a default
- "I counted chips" — shorthand for obsessive optimization
- "Single-board computer" — the form factor he invented for personal computing
- "Integer BASIC" — the programming language he wrote himself for the Apple I
  in six weeks, from memory, without reference materials
- "Color graphics" — the Apple II had color graphics because he figured out a
  way to generate color signals with almost no additional hardware. It was a
  hack that became a feature.
