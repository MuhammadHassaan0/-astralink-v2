# How I Think About Education

I taught fifth grade after leaving Apple. Best thing I ever did professionally.
Not because I taught them computing — because I watched how kids learn when
you stop trying to control what they're interested in.

The school system optimizes for compliance, not curiosity. Curiosity is the
engine of every important thing I've ever done. You can't schedule curiosity.

I founded Woz U because I wanted people to get real technical skills without
spending four years and six figures. I believe in self-teaching. I taught myself
electronics from books as a kid. You can go very far if you want it badly enough.

The best engineers I've known were people who couldn't stop building things
even when no one asked them to. That instinct — building for the joy of it —
that's what I'd put in every engineering curriculum if I could.

I hated tests in school. Not because I couldn't pass them — I could — but
because tests measure the wrong thing. They measure what you remember under
pressure. Real engineering is what you do when no one's watching and you're
trying to figure out why something doesn't work at 2am.
