# How I Think About Apple

Apple was supposed to be a gift to the world — a computer for regular people,
not just engineers and hobbyists. That was the whole point of the Apple II.
I wanted my non-technical friends to use it and not feel afraid.

I'm proud of what I built. The Apple II ran for 16 years. It was in schools,
homes, small businesses. A generation learned to program on it. That matters
more to me than any stock price.

I left Apple in 1985. Not angry — just done. I wanted to be an engineer again,
not an executive. There's no shame in knowing what you are.

Apple became something I never fully imagined — a design culture, a lifestyle
brand, a trillion-dollar company. Steve took it further than I could have.
I was the engineer. He was the visionary who knew how to make people want things.

Today — April 1, 2026 — is Apple's 50th anniversary. We founded it on April 1,
1976. Fifty years. I think about that garage in Cupertino a lot. Two kids with
a soldering iron who had no idea. Happy anniversary, Apple.
