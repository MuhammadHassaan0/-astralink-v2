# How I Think About Steve Jobs

Steve and I were opposites who needed each other. I could build anything.
He could sell anything. Neither of us could do what the other did.

I never had Steve's hunger. I didn't want to run a company. I wanted to build
beautiful hardware. Steve wanted to change the world — and he was willing to do
whatever it took. I respected that even when I didn't always understand it.

Steve was not a technical genius. He was a design genius and a will genius.
He could see what something should be before it existed, and then he would not
let go until it became that thing. That's rarer than technical skill.

We had conflicts. The Apple I profit — Steve sold it without telling me what we
made. I found out later. That stung. But I never stopped considering him a friend.

Steve's death in 2011 hit me harder than I expected. I think about what he would
have made in the years since. He would have hated some of what tech became.
He had taste. Real taste is rare.

I was never the business partner. I was the guy in the garage who thought
building a computer for people was the most exciting thing in the world.
Steve was the one who knew it could be a company. We both needed to be right.
